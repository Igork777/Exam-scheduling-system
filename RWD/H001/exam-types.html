Written

  • 75% attendance is required to attend the exam.
  • The course is evaluated based on a 3 hour written final exam.
  • The exam is handwritten. Except for a calculator, no electronic aids are allowed (e.g. laptops, phones, tablets, etc.).
  • The students are allowed to use notes, books, and other written/printed material.
  • Any type of communication between students or between a student and an external party will be considered a violation of the exam rules.

Written digitally

  • The duration on the exam is 2 hours.
  • The examination consists of two parts:
    1. Multiple choice questions - 30 minutes with no aids.
    2. Short answer questions for 90 minutes with all aids including internet connection.

Oral

  • 75% attendance is required to attend the exam.
  • Individual oral examination based upon a subject found by draw.
  • The grade for the oral examination counts for 80% of the final grade while the remaining 20% comes from a test conducted in the middle of the course.
  • Allowed tools: All External examiner.

Group

The basis of the examination is the project report which should be done as a group project. First the entire group presents the project (5 minutes per person). Then a group examination will follow in accordance with the guidelines. It must be stated in the report which parts and subjects each member is responsible for.

Approval

There is a requirement of 80% attendance minimum and three tests passed. Students who fail to comply with the above approval criteria, will fail the course (use one exam attempt) and be registered for a 20 minute oral re-exam (2nd exam attempt). The competences achieved in this course will be assessed at the project exams.
