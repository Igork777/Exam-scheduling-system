Learn about rules for your exams

It is a good idea to familiarize yourself with the examination rules before the examination period so you don't run into any trouble. Here are some ground rules for all the exams.

  • NO mobile phones
  • Absolutely NO cheating (it's really not worth it)
  • If you become ill you must inform the Student Administration immediately - if you are unable to document your illness it will count as 1 exam attempt.

If you are unsure about some of the rules, you should get in touch with one of your supervisors.

If you haven't found what you were looking for, you can find some more specific information below.

Written exams

  • Time and exam room will appear from the exam plan posted in the Schedule section
  • You are not allowed to bring mobile phones â€“ if you do, they must be handed over to the invigilator before the start of the exam.
  • You can only leave the exam room when accompanied by the invigilator. If you need to go to the toilet, you must use the toilet shown to you by the invigilator. You cannot go outside, to the canteen or elsewhere. Smoking is not permitted.
  • You are not allowed to contact the other examinees during the test. You can only contact the invigilator by raising your hand. Please note that the invigilator is not allowed to answer any questions concerning the assignment, the exam paper or the grading. There must be complete silence the last 30 minutes of the exam. This means that you are not allowed to print, pack your things or leave the room before the exam has ended.

Oral exams

  • Time and exam room will appear from the exam plan posted in the Schedule section
  • Show up 15 minutes before so that you can fill the spot if a fellow student is absent, thereby securing that the exam may be conducted without breaks. If you are late you can only participate if the lecturer and external examiner allow you to participate.
  • The number of exam questions is proportional to the number of students and the questions cover the syllabus to a reasonable extent.
  • When the test starts all questions must be laid out and at no time during the test can less than 3 questions be available to choose from. You draw a question, yourself.
  • The lecturer will examine you. The external examiner can only ask clarifying questions. Under normal circumstances the lecturer is not allowed to stop the examination without consent from the external examiner.

Semester exams

  • Time and exam room will appear from the exam plan posted in the Schedule section
  • Show up 15 minutes before so that you are ready to present your project when the exam time starts.
  • In project exams where the assessment covers several courses, one lecturer per course will typically participate.
  • The exam will take place in a room with projector. Bring your own computer.
